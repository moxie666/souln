" I will never have
glass skin"

Since the age of 8 I had horrendous acne. At 13 I took Accutane and all that went away but I was left with scars and some hyper pigmentation. People were so mean, boys used to call me ugly and make fun of me. I just felt ugly all the time. I couldn't handle the pressure and so I left school.

During the years I was out of school I put on a lot of weight and managed to get diabetes. I got acne again, but it wasn't as bad as the first time, I just have a lot of hyper pigmentation. It's now been 2 months since I've been diligently working out and eating right and I don't get acne any more, but the hyper pigmentation is very persistent. I've been applying doctor prescribed hydroquinone on the affected areas twice daily since the beginning of this month, but I'm not seeing much of a difference. Yesterday, I noticed a lot of visible bumps (they're smooth to the touch but visible in very bright areas, I'm not sure what they're called) so I didn't use the hydroquinone yesterday.

I just want perfect skin so badly. I know I'll probably never get rid of the scars but I want the hyper pigmentation on my face to disappear so badly.

Body Positivity

Body positivity refers to the assertion that all people deserve to have a positive body image, regardless of how society and popular culture view ideal shape, size, and appearance.

Some of the goals of the body positivity movement include:

  • challenging how society views the body
  • promoting the acceptance of all bodies
  • helping people build confidence and acceptance of their own bodies
  • addressing unrealistic body standards
Body positivity is not just about challenging how society views people based upon their physical size and shape, however. It also recognizes that judgments are often made based on race, gender, sexuality, and disability.

Body positivity also aims to help people understand how popular media messages contribute to the relationship that people have with their bodies, including how they feel about food, exercise, clothing, health, identity, and self-care. By better understanding the effect that such influences have, the hope is that people can develop a healthier and more realistic relationship with their bodies.
